<topic>
	<title>What is a printer? </title>
	<text> 
A printer is a peripheral device that accepts text and graphics output from a computer and transfers them to paper. The most common printers are inkjet and laser printers.
	</text>

<topic>
	<title> Connecting the Printer to the System Unit </title>
	<text> 
	The following instructions are applicable to printers with USB connectors.

	Procedure:
		Position your printer next to the system unit.
		Connect the USB cable of the printer to a free USB port of the system unit.
		Plug the power cord of the printer to a power outlet.
		Turn on the printer.
	</text>
	<link>//www.youtube.com/embed/1ZI9CVxU2_Y</link>
</topic>
<topic>
	<title> Installing the printer driver </title>
	<text> 
Procedure:

Insert the CD containing the printer driver into the CDROM.
In Windows Explorer, browse and select the drive containing the device driver.
Double click on setup.exe or the appropriate executable file.
Follow the onscreen instructions.
	</text>
	<link>//www.youtube.com/embed/1ZI9CVxU2_Y</link>
</topic>

<topic>
	<title> Performing print test </title>
	<text> 
Procedure: 


Go to Control Panel
Double Click on Printer and Devices icon
Right click on the printer you just installed and select Properties
Click on the Print Test Page button
If there are no errors, the printer will print a test page containing information about the printer
	</text>
